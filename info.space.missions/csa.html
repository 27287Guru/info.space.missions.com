Radarsat Constellation Mission (RCM)
Launched in June 2019, RCM is a constellation of three Earth observation satellites that provide C-band synthetic aperture radar (SAR) data for applications such as disaster management, environmental monitoring, and maritime surveillance.
Canadarm series
The Canadarm and Canadarm2 are robotic arms developed by Canada for space missions. Canadarm was used on the Space Shuttle program from 1981 to 2011, while Canadarm2 is currently used on the International Space Station (ISS) for maintenance, assembly, and docking activities.
Alouette and ISIS satellites
Alouette-1, launched in 1962, was Canada's first satellite and the world's first satellite dedicated to studying the ionosphere. It laid the foundation for Canada's expertise in satellite technology. The ISIS (International Satellites for Ionospheric Studies) program continued this legacy with subsequent satellites.
MOST (Microvariability and Oscillations of STars) satellite
Launched in 2003, MOST is Canada's first space telescope. It is designed for precise photometric observations of stars, studying their variability and oscillations to understand stellar astrophysics.
CASSIOPE (Cascade SmallSat and Ionospheric Polar Explorer) mission
Launched in 2013, CASSIOPE is a dual-purpose satellite. It hosts scientific instruments for studying the ionosphere and also serves as a technology demonstration platform for advanced communication technologies, such as the ePOP (enhanced Polar Outflow Probe) instrument.
