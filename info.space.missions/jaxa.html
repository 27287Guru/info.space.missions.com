Hayabusa and Hayabusa2 missions
Hayabusa (MUSES-C): Launched in 2003, Hayabusa was the first mission to return samples from an asteroid to Earth. It successfully returned samples from the asteroid Itokawa in 2010, despite numerous challenges during its mission. Hayabusa2: Launched in 2014, Hayabusa2 arrived at the asteroid Ryugu in 2018. It deployed multiple rovers and the Mobile Asteroid Surface Scout (MASCOT) lander to study the asteroid's surface. Hayabusa2 collected samples from Ryugu in 2019 and is scheduled to return them to Earth in December 2020.
Akatsuki (Venus Climate Orbiter)
Launched in 2010, Akatsuki is a Venus orbiter mission designed to study the atmosphere and climate of Venus. After a failed attempt to enter Venus' orbit in 2010, Akatsuki successfully entered orbit around Venus in 2015 and continues to study the planet's atmosphere and weather patterns.
Kibo (Japanese Experiment Module)
Kibo is Japan's contribution to the International Space Station (ISS). Launched in multiple stages starting in 2008, Kibo is a pressurized module that serves as a scientific research laboratory for experiments conducted in microgravity.
ASTER (Advanced Spaceborne Thermal Emission and Reflection Radiometer)
ASTER is a high-resolution imaging instrument onboard NASA's Terra satellite, developed in collaboration with JAXA and METI (Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry of Japan). ASTER provides detailed maps of Earth's surface temperature, reflectance, and elevation for environmental monitoring, natural disaster assessment, and resource management.
IKAROS (Interplanetary Kite-craft Accelerated by Radiation Of the Sun)
Launched in 2010, IKAROS is a demonstration mission of solar sail technology. It successfully deployed a solar sail in space, demonstrating the feasibility of using solar pressure for propulsion in interplanetary travel.
