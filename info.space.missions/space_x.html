Falcon 1 Flight 4 (2008)
Mission: First privately developed liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit. Significance: Demonstrated SpaceX's capability to develop and launch a rocket into orbit, marking a significant milestone in commercial spaceflight.
Dragon COTS Demo Flight 1 (2010)
Mission: First privately developed spacecraft to be recovered successfully from orbit. Significance: Established SpaceX's ability to safely deliver cargo to and from the International Space Station (ISS), under NASA's Commercial Orbital Transportation Services (COTS) program.
Falcon 9 Flight 20 (2015)
Mission: To explore the surface of Mars and search for signs of past life. Significance: The Mars rovers have significantly advanced our understanding of the Martian surface and geology. Notably, the Curiosity rover, which landed in 2012, has provided evidence that Mars once had conditions suitable for life. The Perseverance rover, landing in 2021, is tasked with searching for signs of ancient life and collecting samples for future return to Earth.
Crew Dragon Demo-2 (2020)
Mission: First crewed test flight of the Crew Dragon spacecraft. Significance: Successfully transported NASA astronauts to and from the ISS, marking the return of crewed spaceflight capability to the United States since the retirement of the Space Shuttle in 2011.
Starship SN15 Flight (2021)
Mission: To observe the universe in the infrared spectrum and study the formation of stars and galaxies. Significance: Launched on December 25, 2021, the JWST is the most powerful space telescope ever built, succeeding the Hubble Space Telescope. It aims to look deeper into the universe's history, observe the formation of the first galaxies, and examine the atmospheres of exoplanets.
