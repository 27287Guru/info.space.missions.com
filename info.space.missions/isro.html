Chandrayaan-1
Launched in October 2008, Chandrayaan-1 was India's first mission to the Moon. It made history by discovering water molecules on the lunar surface using its Moon Impact Probe and also provided valuable data on the Moon's composition.
Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission)
Launched in November 2013, Mangalyaan is India's first interplanetary mission. It successfully entered Mars' orbit in September 2014, making India the first Asian country to reach Martian orbit and the fourth space agency in the world to do so.
GSAT series of communication satellites
The GSAT (Geosynchronous Satellite) series consists of numerous satellites launched by ISRO to provide communication, broadcasting, and broadband services across India. These satellites have significantly enhanced India's communication infrastructure.
PSLV-C37 mission (104 satellites in one launch)
In February 2017, ISRO's PSLV-C37 mission set a world record by launching 104 satellites into orbit in a single mission. This demonstrated ISRO's capability to handle complex missions and its cost-effective launch services.
IRNSS (Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System)
Also known as NavIC (Navigation with Indian Constellation), IRNSS is India's regional satellite navigation system designed to provide accurate position information to users in India and the surrounding region. It aims to provide reliable navigation services for both civilian and military purposes.
