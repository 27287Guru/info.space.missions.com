Sputnik 1
Launched on October 4, 1957, Sputnik 1 was the world's first artificial satellite and marked the beginning of the space age. It orbited Earth for three weeks, transmitting radio signals that were received around the world.
Vostok 1
Launched on April 12, 1961, Vostok 1 carried cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin, who became the first human to orbit the Earth. This historic mission made Gagarin an international hero and demonstrated Soviet space supremacy during the early years of the Space Race.
Luna series
The Luna program consisted of a series of robotic spacecraft missions launched between 1959 and 1976. Luna 2, launched in 1959, was the first spacecraft to reach the Moon, impacting its surface. Luna 9, launched in 1966, was the first spacecraft to achieve a soft landing on the Moon and transmit images from the lunar surface.
Salyut and Mir space stations
Salyut program: Launched between 1971 and 1986, the Salyut space stations were the world's first space stations. They served as platforms for scientific research and international collaboration in space. Mir: Launched in 1986 and operated until 2001, Mir was a modular space station that served as a microgravity research laboratory and hosted long-duration human space missions. It was a precursor to the International Space Station (ISS).
ExoMars missions
ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter (TGO): Launched in 2016 in collaboration with the European Space Agency (ESA), TGO is studying the Martian atmosphere to understand its composition, particularly focusing on methane and other trace gases that could indicate biological or geological activity. ExoMars Rover (Roscosmos is a partner): Planned to launch in 2022 (as of my last update), the ExoMars Rover will search for signs of past or present life on Mars, drill below the surface to collect samples, and analyze them for organic compounds.
