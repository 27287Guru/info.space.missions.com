Rosetta (2004-2016)
Mission: Orbiter and lander mission to study comet 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko. Significance: Rosetta successfully orbited and deployed the Philae lander, providing detailed insights into the composition and evolution of comets and their possible role in the origin of life on Earth.
Hubble Space Telescope (ESA/NASA collaboration)
Mission: Space telescope for deep space observation across multiple wavelengths. Significance: ESA has been a key partner in the Hubble Space Telescope (launched in 1990), which has revolutionized our understanding of the universe by capturing detailed images of distant galaxies, nebulae, and other celestial objects.
Gaia (2013-present)
Mission: Space observatory to create a 3D map of the Milky Way galaxy. Significance: Gaia has been mapping billions of stars with unprecedented precision, providing insights into the galaxy's structure, formation, and evolution.
ExoMars (ongoing)
Mission: Robotic exploration of Mars in collaboration with Roscosmos (Russia). Significance: ExoMars aims to search for signs of past and present life on Mars, with the Trace Gas Orbiter (TGO) currently in operation around Mars and the Rosalind Franklin rover planned for future exploration.
Copernicus Programme (ongoing)
Mission: Earth observation programme with Sentinel satellites. Significance: ESA's Copernicus programme monitors the Earth's environment with a fleet of satellites, providing crucial data on climate change, natural disasters, and urban development for policy makers, scientists, and citizens.
