New Shepard Suborbital Program
New Shepard NS-15 (April 14, 2021): This was a significant milestone as it was the first human flight of the New Shepard spacecraft. Jeff Bezos, his brother Mark Bezos, aviation pioneer Wally Funk, and Dutch student Oliver Daemen were onboard, marking the first crewed mission for Blue Origin.
New Glenn Orbital Rocket
New Glenn (upcoming): Blue Origin is developing the New Glenn rocket, which is designed to be a heavy-lift orbital launch vehicle capable of carrying payloads and crew to low Earth orbit (LEO) and beyond. It is scheduled for its first flight no earlier than late 2024.
NASA Tipping Point Partnerships
Blue Origin has partnered with NASA under the Tipping Point program to advance key technologies for lunar exploration and space missions. This includes developing a lunar lander under NASA's Artemis program.
Blue Moon Lunar Lander
Blue Moon is Blue Origin's lunar lander designed to deliver cargo payloads to the lunar surface. It aims to support NASA's plans for sustainable lunar exploration and has been proposed as part of NASA's Human Landing System for Artemis missions.
New Shepard Research Flights
Blue Origin has conducted numerous research flights with the New Shepard suborbital vehicle to test technologies, conduct scientific experiments in microgravity, and pave the way for future commercial suborbital space tourism.
